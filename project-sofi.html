Overview

The Problem

During my Product Management internship at SoFi, I identified a gap in how the platform communicated trust to prospective members. SoFi's product pages lacked dynamic, real-time signals of peer activity, such as how many people had recently been approved for a loan or were actively viewing an investment product. SoFi's own internal research showed that increasing social trust correlated with a 20% lift in the likelihood of a member adopting their next paid product. I saw an opening to close that gap through a lightweight, scalable social proof component that could be deployed across multiple product lines and tested rigorously via A/B experimentation.

Process

What I Did

Executed 0 to 1 creation of social proof modules on SoFi.com, two dynamic data display components surfacing real-time metrics (e.g., new account opens, page views) to 1M+ monthly visitors.

Converted focus group insights into prioritized requirements, launching reusable self-service site editing tools adopted by the Marketing department, reducing update turnaround time by 30% and freeing engineering capacity for faster experimentation.

Impact

Results

Drove a 6.75% lift in Invest application start rates across 1M+ monthly visitors.

The Dynamic Social Proof Module shipped successfully and was well-received by the Marketing team, who deployed it across a backlog of additional use cases. Because we built the component to be self-serve, the marketing team can now reuse and extend it across product pages with zero additional engineering lift. What started as two targeted experiments became a scalable platform capability.
