Overview

The Problem

For people with ADHD and other mental health challenges, starting is often the hardest part. It's not laziness or lack of motivation, it's a neurological barrier that makes even simple tasks feel impossibly large. Opening a new tab can become a moment of paralysis: the blank page, the mental weight of everything that needs to get done, and the all-too-familiar spiral into doom scrolling or bedrotting instead. When our team sat down for the MCIT Hackathon 2025, which was themed around Wellness, we kept coming back to this gap.

Process

What I Built

A Chrome extension designed to give users a gentle, joyful start to their day.

Most productivity tools are built for people who are already "unstuck." They assume you can easily create a list of tasks and get started on them. We wanted to design for the moment before that, the moment where someone needs a gentle on-ramp back into their day. To solve this problem, we created Nara. Rather than presenting an overwhelming to-do list, Nara helps you get a head start by suggesting tasks. You open a new tab, pick a focus area to care for that feels manageable (i.e. yourself, your home, your pet) and get five small, achievable tasks tailored to that category. We help users get out of a rut, build a little momentum, and feel good about it.

We also built a hidden custom list mode, accessible by hovering over the temple doors on the landing page. From there, users can write their own tasks and reorder them freely.
